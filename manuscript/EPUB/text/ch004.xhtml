2 Resolving the space-charge problem with electric and magnetic fields

Here we show that the space-charge problem can be solved in a plasma-free process. This process involves only electrons but no ions. It is therefore best characterized as "thermoelectronic". To remove the static space charges, a positively charged gate electrode is inserted into the emitter-collector space to create a potential trough. In a virtually lossless process this trough accelerates the electrons away from the emitter surface and decelerates them as they approach the collector (Fig. 1). A nominally homogeneous magnetic field HH applied along the electron trajectories prevents loss of the electrons to a gate current IgI_g by directing them through holes in the gate on helical paths circling straight axes. This process turns the static space-charge cloud, which previously blocked the electron emission, into a useful output current (Fig. 1b,c). The design is analogous to that of ion thrusters used for spacecraft propulsion.

To investigate the effectiveness of the gate in removing the space charges, we fabricated a set of thermoelectronic generators as model systems (Figs. 2a,b). The function of the generators was furthermore modeled by numerical calculations of the electron emission, space-charge formation and electron trajectories.
