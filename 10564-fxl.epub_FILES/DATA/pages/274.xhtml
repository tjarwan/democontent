5. Sellars’s myth of Jones: In saying “S believes P” we are hypothesizing some
kind of inner state that we conceptualize by analogy with S making an
assertion that P. The supposition that we conceive of inner states by
analogy with assertions involves a kind of agnosticism about the real
psychological nature of the states attributed.
We thus have one proposal (intentional realism) that makes a claim about
the psychological nature of beliefs (but one that is implausible for many
perfectly ordinary belief attributions) and a range of proposals that make
claims about what we are doing in attributing beliefs, and set constraints
on a psychologically realistic account of beliefs without supplying such
an account. Clearly what we should want here is a psychological proposal
that can account for our dispositions to form judgments, the success of the
intentional strategy, the possession of normative commitments, and the
analogy between beliefs and assertions. Mental models help provide such
an account.
First, if we are disposed to judge—quickly, automatically, and confi-
dently—that dogs have kidneys and 117 + 8 = 125, on what basis are we so
disposed? Our discussion of intuitive judgments suggests that, at least in a
wide variety of such cases, the disposition is rooted in the possession of a
mental model containing resources that can generate the judgment with-
out explicit reasoning. I have never before considered whether dogs have
kidneys; but to do so, all I need to do is consult some kind of inner model of
typical animal physiology, and the judgment pops out. I have never added
these two numbers before (or if I have, the result is forgotten), but I have
mastered a model for adding integers, and when I plug these particular
addends into it, the answer pops out. (Note that if we adjusted the example
so that the addends exceeded performance limits—say, twenty-digit num-
bers—your answer to the “do you believe …” question might well be “I
don’t know,” at least until you worked the problem out with pencil and
paper or a calculator.)
