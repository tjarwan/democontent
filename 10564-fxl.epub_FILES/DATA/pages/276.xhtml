Linguistic beings like ourselves can readily transpose many (though not

On my view, the intentional strategy is itself typified by a model—one
for interpreting the behavior of agents. Or perhaps better, the intentional
strategy may be a feature of any number of more particular models (the
core agency system, folk psychology, and more specialized models erected
on the scaffolding these provide) for interpreting agents. It may well be
that it does so, as Sellars suggests, by characterizing agents as though their
behavior were driven by language-like inner states. Like any model, this
sort of model is idealized and answers to a number of pragmatic factors
for its aptness. It is particularly apt in cases in which there are psycho-
logical processes involving language-like representations—including, but
not limited to, thinking and reasoning in a natural language. It distorts
its target more when there are model-based judgments that are not trans-
posed into language or language-like thinking, but it can still latch onto
psychologically real episodes even if it (mistakenly) treats them as though
they were in a language-like medium. It also provides ways of talking about
dispositions and normative commitments based in model-based thinking,
though it does so in a fashion that provides no insight into their format
or dynamics. The real patterns it tracks are emergent from an underlying
model-based dynamics. In my view, it is features of these dynamics that are
its real target—a form of psychological realism that is not a realism about
sentence-like representations.
But the strategy can also be applied, with varying degrees of success, to
systems that lack the same model-based dynamics. In the limiting case, we
can say things such as “the thermostat believed it was too cool and switched
on the furnace.” Statements of this kind can allow us to attain some degree
of predictive success even while greatly distorting the way we represent the
system we are predicting. For Dennett, predictive success would seem to be
the only measure of aptness. For me, it is one among several, and I would
insist on a richer analysis of how an intentional strategy model is and is not
aptly applied to a range of cases instead of a simple prediction-based crite-
rion for what is a “true believer.” In my view, the intentional strategy aims
