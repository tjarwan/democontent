5. In addition to being used for classification and inference, concepts have

part of the phenomenology and logical structure of conceptual thinking.

units in a language.
7. We need to account for how concepts can play a role in language-like
thinking.
A number of possible theoretical models could serve as candidates for
meeting all these constraints. Fully enumerating them and arbitrating
between them is a large project in its own right, which would require a
good deal of both philosophical analysis and empirical testing. This task
would arguably call for a book of its own. What I will attempt to do here is
therefore more modest: to present elements that I think such a model needs
to have, and to suggest, for further discussion and development, several dif-
ferent ways it might be completed. I will do this with reference to diagrams,
which I will build up from simpler to more complex.
