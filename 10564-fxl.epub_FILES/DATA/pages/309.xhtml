cannot merely be that we use the same word, because a single word can be
used to talk about distinct classes of things, like a financial institution and
the side of a riverbed, and in those sorts of cases we have no similar tempta-
tion to suppose that there is only a single concept.
Consider some other examples. There are no doubt a number of distinct
models that deal with cows. People who herd cattle probably have mental
models of how cattle roam about singly and in groups and how to interact
with them to cajole them into going where the herder wishes them to go.
Cattle breeders have mental models of different heritable traits of cows and
how they can be combined through selective breeding. Wildlife ecologists
dealing with open ranges have ecological models involving cattle and their
impact on particular environments, and so on. They all use the word ‘cow’
to talk about the same class of things, and it takes no special mental gym-
nastics to recognize that it is the same class of things that is understood,
in different ways, through the different models. The natural conclusion to
draw would seem to be that there is a single concept,
