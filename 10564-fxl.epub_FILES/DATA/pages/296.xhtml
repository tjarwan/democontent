Brandom’s claim that “it does not follow that they must come in just one
great big package” invites a further distinction, between globally holist the-
ories and “molecularist” (Block 1998) or “localist” (Weiskopf 2009) theo-
ries that define the fundamental semantic unit somewhere between the
individual concept and the entire web of concepts, beliefs, and inferential
commitments:
Mental (or semantic) holism is the doctrine that the identity of a belief content (or
the meaning of a sentence that expresses it) is determined by its place in the web
of beliefs or sentences comprising a whole theory or group of theories. It can be
contrasted with two other views: atomism and molecularism. Molecularism char-
acterizes meaning and content in terms of relatively small parts of the web in a way
that allows many different theories to share those parts. For example, the meaning
of ‘chase’ might be said by a molecularist to be try to catch. Atomism characterizes
meaning and content in terms of none of the web; it says that sentences and beliefs
have meaning or content independently of their relations to other sentences or be-
liefs. (Block 1998)
A model-based cognitive pluralism clearly favors a “molecularist” theory of
semantics. Most debates in this area, however, treat atomism and holism
as the options, and often one theory is touted largely on the grounds that
the other is unacceptable. It is widely argued that molecularist accounts are
not really a viable third alternative, on the grounds that there is allegedly
no way of containing the spread of semantic interrelations short of holism.
And this may well be correct, if we assume that the only relevant units are
concepts, beliefs, and inferences. This, however, is precisely where cogni-
tive pluralism differs from the more familiar theories. A mental model is
a distinct type of psychological unit. Moreover, it is not defined in terms
of interrelationships between concepts, beliefs, and inferences in the
sense of being a kind of construct out of them. It is conceived to be a real
feature of cognitive architecture and as such can provide a principled basis
for constitutive relationships within a model that can be differentiated from
merely epistemic and inferential relationships with things outside it. This
is just the kind of notion the molecularist needs to escape the charge that
there is an unavoidable slippery slope into holism.
