7.9 Conclusion
Mental models provide systematic ways of understanding particular con-
tent domains. A model functions as a unit, with interconnected compo-
nents that track salient features of its target system. This allows us to use
models as internal surrogates for their targets, and to think and reason
about the targets in ways that track their invariant features. A model is
more or less apt to the extent that it accurately reflects the features relevant
to some particular set of theoretical and practical interests. In some ways,
models might better be described as modeling systems, as a model supplies
tools for representing and reasoning about particular situations within its
target domain and generates a set of possible representations of those states,
as well as supplying efficient forms of reasoning about them, such as simu-
lating their dynamics.
