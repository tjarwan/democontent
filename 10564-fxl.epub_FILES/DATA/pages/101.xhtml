The second map, a sinusoidal projection (fig. 6.6), preserves proportions
of areas on the globe, essentially by slicing up the surface of the globe rather
than stretching it. It results in a figure that is initially difficult to orient
to, because of the many discontinuities, which seem counterintuitive. The
sinusoidal projection distorts distance measurements between two points
except those located on the same parallel. However, for purposes of preserv-
ing information about areas, it has advantages over the Mercator projection.
