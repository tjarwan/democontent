Often (and perhaps always) the truth of a claim can be assessed only
once one has fixed what model is in play. Suppose that Smith witnesses a
traffic accident and testifies that the sedan was standing still and the bus
ran into it. In court, it will not count as a refutation of Smith’s testimony if
the opposing counsel points out that, according to our best physical mod-
els, there is no such thing as absolute rest, and hence the sedan could not
have been standing still. What “standing still” means depends on whether
we are adopting a commonsense model in which motion is judged relative
to the face of the earth or a model suited to physics and cosmology. It is the
former that the judge is likely to count as relevant to matters of traffic law,
and likely this was the type of model that Smith had in mind when giving
his testimony.
5.7 Types of Error
Corresponding to the two levels of alethetic assessment (aptness and truth),
there are two ways that representation may go awry. One of these involves
the tokening of a representation that is not the best match available within
the limits of the operative model for tracking whatever it is its function to
track. Asked to give the value of π, I say that it is 17. Or I know about both
cows and horses and misidentify a cow as a horse, perhaps because of bad
lighting. Or I say that the two lines in an illusory figure are of different
lengths when their lengths are equal. In such cases, the representation is
false.
But there is another sort of case, in which a given model does not pos-
sess adequate resources to produce an apt representation at all, and perhaps
I lack (and may not be capable of grasping) a model that can do so. For
example, I can produce only a finite approximation of the value of π, but a
fully accurate representation requires an infinite decimal sequence. Or my
subjective zoological ontology differentiates cows from horses but does not
extend to differentiating different breeds of cattle, so I cannot identify this
cow as a jersey or a Black Angus. Or I find myself unable to represent the
world except in terms of classical objects and thus find the problems and
models of quantum mechanics unfathomable.
