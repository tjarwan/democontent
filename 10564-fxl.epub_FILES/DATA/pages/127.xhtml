Like most models, social models provide a space for possible alternative
events and sequences of events. People don’t always order the same thing
at restaurants. Sometimes they may just go for dessert or drinks. Differ-
ent things happen at different trials, and with a different attorney, a given
case might have resulted in a different outcome. Even a tightly constrained
event like a play allows latitude. Lear must be played mad when he carries
in Cordelia’s body in act 5, scene 3 (though note that it is a dramatic sense
of “must,” not one suggesting physical or logical necessity), and the actors
must say the lines that Shakespeare composed; but one actor might play
Lear weeping, and another might play his madness as having taken him
into a completely dissociative state, thinking that Cordelia merely sleeps
and watching her happily for a time. Like other models, social models pro-
vide a framework for interpreting a particular type of situation, but there are
various ways things might go within that framework. Some of these may
be built into the model itself; others may be like improvisations on it. And,
of course, in any real-world situation there will be many other things going
on as well, some of which might ultimately impact on the aptness of the
model. I might, for example, discover after several minutes in a restaurant
that the seated people are not really diners but actors in a drama. Or I might
walk in on an impromptu rehearsal of King Lear and mistakenly think I am
really witnessing an old man mourning the death of his daughter.
Social models are unlike many other models, however, in the ways they
shape not only our interpretation of the world but also the social world
itself. The behavior of chemical bonds does not change when our models
of it change. But conduct in a courtroom changes when trial law changes
and those changes are appropriated by judges and attorneys; and people in
cultures that lack any understanding of the Japanese tea ceremony do not
engage in that ceremony, however much tea they might consume.
