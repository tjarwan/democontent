5.9 Looking Forward
In the chapters of part 2, I expand on this brief introduction to cognitive
pluralism. I begin with a more concerted intuitive development of the
notion of models in general and mental models in particular, followed
by observations about relationships between mental models. Part 2 con-
cludes with two chapters that consider why a cognitive architecture using
a large number of mental models is a good strategy for developing intelli-
gent organisms, and how mental models might be related to other capaci-
ties of the mind, particularly public language and language-like thinking.
Part 3 then turns to implications of cognitive pluralism for the unity of
understanding, intuitive reasoning, cognitive illusions, epistemology, and
semantics.
