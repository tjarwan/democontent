Relations between Models
8 Relations between Models
In this chapter, I explore five different sorts of relationships that can be
found among different mental models:
1. Two models can be related by degree of abstractness.
2. One model can be a variant on another.
3. One model can be created from another through metaphorical
transposition.
4. Two or more models can be used in tandem to triangulate objects, states
of affairs, or entire domains.
5. Two models can be dissonant with each other in various ways, such as
incommensurability, formal incompatibility, and generating contradic-
tory interpretations or predictions when applied to particular cases.
These are five very different types of relations, and I have combined the dis-
cussions of them into a single chapter chiefly because together they come
to a reasonable chapter length. The discussions of triangulation and of ways
models can be dissonant with one another, while comparatively brief, are
of particular importance, and both will be taken up again in part 3.
