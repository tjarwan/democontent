Concepts within and outside Models
What we need now is a way of combining this picture with a model-based
account of understanding, semantics, and reasoning. To do this, it seems to
me that we need to posit more than one kind of psychological entity. On
the one hand, we need models and things that are components of models,
including the representational types that are related to model-based judg-
ments and reasoning. The latter are what we might have taken to be “con-
cepts” in a purely “residential” model-based account. (Call them CMs, for
the notion of concepts involved in models.) On the other, we need a kind of
entity that stands outside models—a kind of quasi-lexical entity. (Call these
CLs, for a concept in a lexical sense.) Because CLs need to be able to change
their semantic properties, their identity is not fixed by such properties. This
leaves the question of what psychological story we should tell about their
semantic properties, and one natural hypothesis is that they have semantic
properties by dint of being linked in the right ways to CMs inside models.
But because CLs stand outside models, a single CL can have links to multi-
ple CMs in different models. I can have “a single concept”
COW in the sense
of having a single CL that is “involved in” or “linked to” various models
of herding, species biology, prairie ecology, and so on. Such a concept can
also change its semantic properties over time, either by changes in which
models the CL is linked to, or by changes within the models to which it is
linked.
It also seems plausible that it is not CMs but their associated CLs that are
used in language-like thought. CLs may literally be the quasi-lexical units
employed in language-like thinking. Ordinary language may very well, by
default, assign words at the level of CLs, and garden-variety homonymy
(bank/bank) involves an accidental assignment of the same phonetically
typed units of a public language to two different CLs. If we count such
homonyms as different words, a word is composed of an associated pair
consisting of a phonetically typed unit and a CL.
