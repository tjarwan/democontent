12.2 Disunity as a Problem
At some level, most of us—not just philosophers and scientists—find dis-
unities of belief and understanding troubling, and this leads us to seek ways
to unify them. It would be useful to take a moment to examine the nature
of this situation, and of our dissatisfaction with it.
First, we each find ourselves with some number of conflicting and even
contradictory beliefs. Sometimes it is that we believe one thing on one
occasion or when primed in a particular way, and another on another occa-
sion or when primed in a different way. But sometimes we find ourselves,
even upon deep reflection, equally committed to two contrary claims and
do not know how to resolve the tension between them.
Second, we find this situation dissatisfying. It drives us to attempt to
find a way of resolving the contradiction. It is no accident that paradoxes
drive much of the histories of both philosophy and science. This seems to
be a deep and productive feature of human psychology: that when we are
pulled toward two contradictory beliefs, we are spurred to find some way
to resolve the contradiction. Often we succeed, and some of these successes
have been watershed moments in the history of human knowledge. A more
complacent species would have learned far less about the world.
Third, we do not just have conflicting beliefs; we have multiple ways of
framing things through models. (This is a slightly more contentious claim
than the first two, because it involves a bit of theory.) Sometimes this is just
a matter of having different ways of framing things. And sometimes it seems
to amount to something more serious: the different ways of framing things
are in various ways in tension with one another, such as being incommen-
surable or implying contradictory inferences.
Fourth, the disparities between models are also dissatisfying. They are
probably not as immediately troubling as contradictions between beliefs,
except perhaps when they generate contradictory beliefs. Yet something
within us is puzzled and troubled even by having two models that frame
the world in different terms, and seeks to find some way of bringing them
into some deeper harmony.
