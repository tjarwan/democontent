6.4 Program Code and Flowcharts
In programming and engineering, processes are often modeled by flow-
charts (fig. 6.10). These are two-dimensional representations of function-
ally defined processes and the relations between them. These relations are
both logical and dynamic. They are dynamic in that arrows in the flowchart
represent a chronological sequence of processing. They are logical in that
the flowchart includes branching points where the actual behavior of the
program depends on particular conditions, understood in Boolean terms.
For example, at a particular place in a program, if a variable V has a positive
value, that value may be displayed on the screen, but if the value is nega-
tive, it may first be multiplied by –1 and then displayed.
