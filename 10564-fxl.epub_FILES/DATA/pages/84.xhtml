Models in perceptual modules: Modules used in perception, such as layers
of V4 involved in color perception, may also be viewed as representa-
tional systems. A system for color, for example, produces arrays of color
representations in a retinotopic map, tracking arrangements of chro-
matic properties within the visual field. Such a model involves a spatial
arrangement of units, with an internal geometry and topology, and each
unit is capable of a range of states modeled by the color solid. Areas later
in the perceptual stream used to represent spatial geometry provide a
model describable in terms of geometric rules that is used for represent-
ing objects in space.
A representational system defines a possibility space for possible token
representations. A particular gravitational model rigorously defines a way
of thinking or speaking about the locations, motions, and dynamic interac-
tions of bodies in space over time. This, in turn, provides the resources for
describing a particular set of bodies (e.g., the sun and the earth) in terms of
position, mass, and inertia, and for calculating the resultant gravitational
component of their dynamics. A restaurant script defines a set of possible
events and actions (and thoughts and utterances about them) that are on
script. This provides the resources for describing, understanding, and act-
ing appropriately in a variety of particular occasions of dining in which
different specific things happen within the constraints of the script. The
architecture of a layer of V4 defines a possible set of visual representations
of colored environments, and perhaps of chromatic experiences. Particular
chromatic experiences can then be located with respect to this space. A par-
ticular utterance or judgment about a gravitational situation picks out one
possibility from such a possibility space, and likewise a judgment, utter-
ance, or perception about events in a restaurant or a chromatic experience.
In some cases, a representational token will pick out a possibility at the
most fine-grained level: for example, at any one time, a personâ€™s subjective
color array has fully determinate values. In other cases, a representational
token will narrow the possibility space while leaving some things indeter-
minate: for example, a description of the sun and the earth within a par-
ticular gravitational model need not affirm or deny the existence of other
bodies with mass, and the description of position and mass may be given to
a finite approximation, although the model theoretically allows for indefi-
nitely more exact descriptions.
