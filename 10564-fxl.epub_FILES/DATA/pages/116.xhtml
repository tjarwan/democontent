Models and Modeling Systems
The example we have been exploring suggests a distinction between mod-
els and modeling systems. A particular figure built from the modeling kit is
a model of a water molecule, but the kit itself is not in the same sense a
model of something in particular. Rather, it is a kind of system for forming
models of more specific types of things. Moreover, at least in some cases, it
seems most natural to say that “models” like that of an individual molecule
are representations, but modeling systems are not representations but rep-
resentational systems.
I think there is something deeply right and crucially important here:
that modeling involves systems that are not themselves representations but
provide the resources for generating representations. However, I argue in
chapter 8 that there is not a hard-and-fast distinction between models and
modeling systems: one and the same thing can be a model (and hence per-
haps a representation) of something in one context and a generative system
for modeling, say, states of that thing in other contexts. For example, an
anatomical model of the sort used in medicine is in one sense a model of
human anatomy; but it can also be used as a framework for modeling more
specific things, like the effects of an injury or the possible ways of making
a surgical intervention.
